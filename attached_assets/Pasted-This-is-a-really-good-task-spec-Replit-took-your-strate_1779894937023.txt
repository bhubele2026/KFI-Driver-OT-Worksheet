Two small things to push back on
1. The grading rubric needs more structure than "useful / noisy / wrong / missed-something."
Those four labels conflate two different axes: was the verdict correct (right finding identified) AND was it useful (the dispatcher would have acted on it). A finding can be technically correct but operationally useless ("upload contains 199 punches" — yes, true, who cares). And a finding can be useful but slightly wrong ("hours look high for Driver X" when actually it's Driver Y).
Recommend a 2×2 instead:
Acted-worthySkippedCorrectTrue positive — verdict caught real issue dispatcher would have acted onNoise — verdict was true but didn't matterWrongFalse positive that would have misled dispatcherHarmless wrong
Plus a separate field: "Did Claude miss anything the dispatcher caught manually?" (false negatives).
That gives you four labels per finding (with a missed-things tally separate). A prompt is good when most findings are true-positive-acted and there are few misses.
2. Five or six finding kinds is suspiciously specific. What are they?
Replit says "Keep the schema deliberately small for v1 — five or six finding kinds at most." That's the right discipline but the contents of those five-six finding kinds aren't specified, and they should be before the prompt is written. Otherwise the prototype produces freeform findings and the grading session devolves into "is this what we meant?"
Worth pinning down upfront. Off the top of my head, here are five kinds worth proposing:

extraction_completeness — Did extraction get everything from the source file, or were rows dropped? Examples: "5 rows in source file not in preview"
roster_match_quality — Are the badge→driver matches likely right? "3 badges auto-matched, 1 may need review"
hours_anomaly — Per-driver totals vs. historical pattern. "Driver X at 47h, normally 38-42"
missing_or_new_driver — Drivers expected but missing, or new badges never seen before. "Driver Y absent from this week's upload, was present last 4 weeks"
structural_concern — File-level oddities. "File spans 8 days, expected 7"; "Customer name in file doesn't match selected customer"

Pin these to the spec before the prompt gets written, otherwise every retry of the prototype includes prompt drift on top of prompt iteration.
